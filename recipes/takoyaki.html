Takoyaki

Description

Takoyaki is a ball-shaped Japanese snack made of a wheat flour-based batter and cooked in a special molded pan. It is typically filled with minced or diced octopus, tempura scraps, pickled ginger, and green onion.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup katsuobushi
  • 2 green onions/scallions
  • 1 tbsp pickled red ginger
  • 4.2 oz octopus sashimi
  • 2 tbsp neutral-flavored oil
  • 1/3 cup tempura scraps

Takoyaki Batter

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups dashi

Toppings

  • 1/2 cup takoyaki sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • katsuobushi
  • aonori
  • pickled red ginger

Steps

  1. Gather ingredients

  2. To Prepare the Filling

    1. Grind the katsuobushi into a fine powder
    2. Cut the green onions into fine slices and mince the red pickled ginger
    3. Cut the octopus into 1/2 inch bite-sized pieces

  3. To Make the Takoyaki Batter

    1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and kosher salt and whisk it all together.
    2. Add the eggs, soy sauce, and dashi.
    3. Whisk it all together until well blended and transfer the batter to a measuring cup with a handle.

  4. To Cook the Takoyaki

    1. Heat the takoyaki pan to over medium heat.
    2. Using a brush, generously oil the pan's rounded chambers and flat surface on the top.
    3. Once smoke is rising from the pan, pour the batter to fill the chambers.
    4. Add 1-3 octopus pieces and sprinkle on top the katsuobushi powder
    5. Sprinkle the tenkasu, green onion, and pickled red ginger on top.
    6. After 3 minutes or so rotate each piece 90 degrees toward the bottom of the pan.
    7. After 4 minutes or so rotate it back.
    8. Swap the balls around so they brown evenly for the next 4 minutes.
  5. To serve: put on plate and drizzle takoyaki sauce or Japanese mayo on top.
  6. Best served immediately but be careful as they are very hot inside!
